Hiking Adventures in Utah

Jump to Top Hike Suggestions

Utah, known for its stunning national parks and diverse landscapes, offers some of the best hiking opportunities in the United States. From the red rocks of the south to the mountainous terrain of the north, there's a trail for everyone.

Top 3 Hike Suggestions

  1. The Narrows (Zion National Park)

    Hike through the Virgin River in a stunning slot canyon. Be prepared to get wet!

    • What to Bring:
    • Closed-toe water shoes
    • Hiking stick/pole
    • Dry bag for electronics
    • Plenty of water
  2. Delicate Arch Trail (Arches National Park)

    A moderately challenging hike leading to Utah's most iconic natural arch. Best viewed at sunrise or sunset.

    • What to Bring:
    • Sunscreen and hat
    • Sturdy hiking boots
    • Headlamp (if hiking for sunrise/sunset)
    • Camera
    • At least 2 liters of water per person
  3. Navajo Loop and Queen's Garden Trail (Bryce Canyon National Park)

    Descend into the amphitheater filled with unique hoodoo formations. A relatively easy but spectacular trail.

    • What to Bring:
    • Good walking shoes
    • Water
    • Layers of clothing (can be cool in the canyon)
